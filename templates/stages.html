Five Stages of Alzheimer's Disease

Stage 1: Preclinical Stage

No Impairment
Duration: Years or decades

Key Features:

  • No impairment
  • Changes in the brain may be occurring, but they will go unnoticed
  • Individual is unaware of any changes

Stage 2: Mild Cognitive Impairment

Mild
Duration: Several years

Key Features:

  • Noticeable memory problems, but not severe enough to interfere with daily life
  • Forgetfulness, difficulty concentrating, and trouble finding words
  • May be noticed by family members or close friends

Stage 3: Mild Dementia

Moderate
Duration: Several years

Key Features:

  • Memory loss becomes more apparent and begins to interfere with daily life
  • Confusion about time or place, difficulty with complex tasks, and changes in personality
  • May require assistance with some activities

Stage 4: Moderate Dementia

Advanced
Duration: 2-10 years

Key Features:

  • Significant memory loss and cognitive decline
  • Requires assistance with daily activities such as dressing, bathing, and eating
  • May grow restless or agitated; some may display aggressive behavior

Stage 5: Severe Dementia

Severe
Duration: 1-3 years

Key Features:

  • Severe memory loss and cognitive decline
  • Significant difficulty with communication and understanding
  • Complete dependence on others for daily activities and personal care
